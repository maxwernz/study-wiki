# Working Memory and the Prefrontal Cortex

## Short definition

Working memory (WM) is the ability to **hold a small amount of information "in mind" and
manipulate it** over seconds, to guide behaviour when the relevant information is no longer
in front of you. It is the component of [[Executive Functions]] that keeps goals and
intermediate results available.

## Intuition

WM is the brain's **mental whiteboard**. When you multiply 17 × 4 in your head, you scribble
"17 × 4 = 68" on the whiteboard, hold "60-something… carry…" while you finish, then erase it.
The board is **small** (a few items), **temporary** (it fades fast unless you keep refreshing
it), and crucially it lets you work on things that aren't physically present. A waiter taking
a five-person order without writing it down is running WM; so is following the thread of this
sentence while you reach its end.

## Explanation

**Origin of the term.** "Working memory" enters psychology via Miller, Galanter & Pribram
(1960) — the memory we use to **execute Plans**, where interrupted plans are held as
"intentions" until we can resume them. Baddeley & Hitch (1974) gave the influential
multi-component model: a **central executive** (attentional controller) plus storage
buffers (phonological loop, visuospatial sketchpad, later the episodic buffer).

**The modern reframing (D'Esposito & Postle 2015).** The contemporary cognitive-neuroscience
view shifts away from "dedicated storage boxes" toward an **attention-based, state-dependent**
account:

- Information is held in WM by **directing attention to internal representations** — whether
  those are long-term-memory codes (letters, digits, words), sensory traces, or motor plans.
- Therefore WM content is represented in the **same posterior/sensory regions that process
  that content in the first place** (the "sensorimotor recruitment" idea): remembering a
  visual grating reactivates visual cortex; this is what multivariate fMRI decoding finds.
- The **prefrontal cortex is the controller, not the store**. PFC exerts top-down control by
  **biasing the salience** of the maintained representations and **adjudicating among
  competing, context-dependent rules** — D'Esposito & Postle's memorable phrase is the
  **"control of the controller."** This control emerges from a PFC–striatal loop gated by
  ascending **dopaminergic** signals (dopamine sets when to update vs. protect WM contents).

So the headline shift: PFC's role in WM is **executive/control**, while the *content* lives
in distributed, modality-specific cortex.

**Lesion evidence for sub-region specialisation (Müller et al. 2002).** Human PFC subdivides
into at least: **dorsolateral PFC (DLPFC; BA 9, 46, 9/46)**, **ventrolateral PFC (BA 44, 45,
47)**, and **ventromedial/orbital PFC (BA 10, 11, 12)**. Two classic theories competed for
how they map onto WM:

- **"Type of information" (domain) theory (Goldman-Rakic):** dorsal PFC = **spatial**
  content, ventral PFC = **object** content — an upward extension of the posterior
  dorsal ("where") / ventral ("what") visual streams.
- **"Type of process" theory:** dorsal vs ventral PFC differ by **operation**
  (manipulation/monitoring vs simple maintenance), not by stimulus category.

Müller, Machado & Knight tested focal-lesion patients and found that **small** lesions
confined to dorsal lateral PFC *or* to ventromedial orbital PFC did **not** impair
maintenance/monitoring of object or spatial information; only **larger lesions spanning both
ventral and dorsal lateral PFC** impaired WM, and then **spatial deficits were worse than
object deficits**. The take-home: lateral-PFC contributions to WM are **distributed** (not
cleanly boxed by sub-region), with a relative bias such that spatial WM is more vulnerable —
partial support for the where/what idea but against a strict anatomical dichotomy.

## Worked example — delayed-response task

The canonical WM probe: show a cue (a location or an object), impose a **delay** with the cue
gone, then require a response based on the remembered cue. In monkeys, DLPFC neurons show
**persistent firing across the blank delay** — the physiological signature of holding
information online. Lesioning PFC abolishes correct delayed responses while leaving immediate
(no-delay) responses intact, which is the logic Müller et al. apply in humans: a deficit that
appears **only when a delay/manipulation is required** isolates the WM/control contribution
from perception or motor ability.

## Formal definition / equations

No defining equation. Two quantitative facts worth remembering: WM **capacity** for visual
objects is famously limited (~3–4 items, Cowan's *k*), and maintenance is **active** (it
decays without continued attention/refreshing) rather than a passive store.

## Role in this class or project

Working memory is one of the **four core components of [[Executive Functions]]** (Rabinovici's
scheme). It is also the substrate other executive operations run on — you cannot plan, shift
set, or exercise self-control without holding the goal online — so it links forward to
[[Hierarchical Cognitive Control]] and [[Value-Based Decision Making and the vmPFC]]
(DLPFC supplying the maintained goal that biases vmPFC value signals).

## Exam, assignment, or project relevance

Know: the **central-executive / multi-component** model vs the **attention-based
state-dependent** model; **PFC = controller, posterior cortex = store** ("control of the
controller", dopamine gating); the **domain ("where/what") vs process** debate and that
**Müller's lesion data favour distributed lateral-PFC function** with spatial > object
vulnerability; and the **delayed-response logic** for isolating WM.

## Common confusions

- **WM ≠ short-term storage.** WM implies **manipulation/control**, not just passive holding;
  the passive buffer is only one piece.
- **"WM is stored in the PFC."** Modern view: content is stored in **posterior modality-
  specific cortex**; PFC **controls** it.
- **Strict dorsal=spatial / ventral=object mapping is too clean** — human lesion data show
  distributed lateral-PFC involvement (Müller et al.).
- **Persistent delay activity ≠ proof of a "storage buffer"** — it can equally reflect
  attention/control signals under the state-dependent account.
