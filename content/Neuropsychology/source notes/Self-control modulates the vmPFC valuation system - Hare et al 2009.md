# Self-control involves modulation of the vmPFC valuation system (Hare et al. 2009)

## TL;DR

In dieters choosing foods, **vmPFC encodes goal value** in everyone; **self-control = DLPFC
modulating that vmPFC value signal** so it incorporates **health**, not just **taste**. DLPFC
engages specifically when self-control is exercised and correlates with vmPFC.

## Topic context

The mechanistic link between the lateral control system and the ventromedial value system on
[[Value-Based Decision Making and the vmPFC]]; explains willpower without a separate "value
centre."

## Problem / question

What are the neural processes underlying self-control, and how do they interact with the
brain's valuation circuitry?

## Approach / method

fMRI while self-reported **dieters** made real eat/don't-eat decisions about foods varying in
**taste** and **health**, after separately rating each attribute (health block, taste block,
decision block).

## Key findings

- **vmPFC activity tracked the goal value** of the chosen food in all subjects.
- In **non-self-controllers**, the vmPFC signal reflected **taste only**; in **successful
  self-controllers**, it reflected **taste *and* health**.
- **DLPFC activity increased when subjects exercised self-control** and **correlated with
  (modulated)** the vmPFC signal.
- So self-control = DLPFC re-weighting the vmPFC value computation toward long-term goals.
