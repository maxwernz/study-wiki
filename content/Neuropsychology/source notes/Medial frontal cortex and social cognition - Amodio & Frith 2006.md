# Meeting of minds: the medial frontal cortex and social cognition (Amodio & Frith 2006)

## TL;DR

The **medial frontal cortex (MFC)** is the integrative hub of the social-cognition network
(with TPJ, STS, temporal poles), organised along a **caudal→rostral gradient**: posterior
action-monitoring → orbital value monitoring → anterior frontopolar **mentalising** (thinking
about minds).

## Topic context

The framework for [[Medial Frontal Cortex and Social Cognition]]; shows social cognition reuses
the same medial-frontal monitoring/value/abstraction machinery as the rest of the EF lecture.

## Problem / question

What is the role of the medial frontal cortex in social cognition, and how is its function
organised?

## Approach / method

Review integrating neuroimaging + anatomy to propose a theoretical model of MFC function across
social-cognitive processes.

## Key findings

- Social cognition engages a **network**: MFC (incl. ACC), TPJ, STS, temporal poles — with
  **MFC as the special integrative node**.
- MFC organised along a **posterior→anterior gradient**: posterior/dorsal **action & outcome
  monitoring** → orbital **value** monitoring → anterior rostral (frontopolar, BA 9/10)
  **representation of mental states** (self and others — mentalising/theory of mind).
- This mirrors the abstraction gradient seen in cognitive control.
