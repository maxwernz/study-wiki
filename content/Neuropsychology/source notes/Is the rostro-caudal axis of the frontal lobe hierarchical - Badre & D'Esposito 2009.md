# Is the rostro-caudal axis of the frontal lobe hierarchical? (Badre & D'Esposito 2009)

## TL;DR

A review arguing the frontal lobe is organised along a **rostro-caudal abstraction gradient**
(anterior = abstract goals, posterior = concrete actions) and that this is a true **hierarchy**:
more-anterior regions exert **asymmetric top-down control** over more-posterior ones.

## Topic context

The synthesis page for [[Hierarchical Cognitive Control]]; distinguishes a mere *gradient* from
a genuine *control hierarchy*. Source of the "make a sandwich" abstraction analogy.

## Problem / question

Is the rostro-caudal functional gradient just a map of abstraction, or do anterior regions
**dominate** posterior ones (the defining property of a hierarchy)?

## Approach / method

Review integrating anatomy, single-unit physiology, and neuroimaging evidence on rostro-caudal
organisation and inter-regional influence.

## Key findings

- Action control is naturally **hierarchical** (abstract goal → subgoals → concrete motor
  acts), and this maps onto a **rostral (abstract) → caudal (concrete)** frontal gradient.
- Beyond a gradient, evidence supports **asymmetric top-down dominance**: anterior regions
  influence posterior processing more than vice versa → a real **processing hierarchy**.
- Anatomy/physiology show systematic rostro-caudal patterns consistent with this.

## Limitations / open questions

The asymmetric-dominance claim was, at the time, suggestive rather than conclusive; "abstraction"
is defined differently across the studies reviewed.
