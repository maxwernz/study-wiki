# A right perisylvian neural network for human spatial orienting (Karnath 2009, in Gazzaniga's *The Cognitive Neurosciences IV*)

## TL;DR

The same anatomical "shape" of network — tightly interconnected cortex straddling the
sylvian fissure — exists in **both** hemispheres, but serves **different functions**:
**language & praxis on the left**, **spatial orienting on the right**. This is why right
perisylvian damage causes neglect while the homologous left damage causes aphasia/apraxia.

## Topic context

Provides the detailed white-matter anatomy behind the perisylvian-network account of
neglect (this is the chapter that Fig. 2 of the 2012 anatomy review is drawn from).

## Method / argument structure

Synthesis of tract-tracing, myelin staining, and diffusion imaging (DTI/DSI), plus
lesion, TMS, intra-operative stimulation, and fMRI evidence.

## Key findings

- **The network's three cortical hubs** — inferior parietal lobule (IPL), ventrolateral
  frontal cortex, superior/middle temporal cortex (+insula) — are linked by:
  - IPL ↔ ventrolateral frontal: **SLF II, SLF III, SOF**
  - ventrolateral frontal ↔ superior/middle temporal: **arcuate fasciculus (AF),
    extreme capsule / inferior occipitofrontal fasciculus (EmC/IOF)**
  - superior temporal ↔ IPL: **middle longitudinal fasciculus (MdLF), EmC/IOF**
- **Left = language/praxis, right = spatial orienting.** Aphasia almost never follows
  right perisylvian damage, as rarely as neglect follows left damage. The lateralization
  of spatial orienting to the right is a human specialization (in monkeys the function is
  bilateral).
- **Not (mainly) a disconnection syndrome.** In 140 right-hemisphere strokes, only a small
  fraction of the neglect-predictive lesion overlapped white-matter tracts (e.g. 7% SLF,
  8% IOF, 13% SOF); **89–97% of the lesion volume hit grey matter** (temporal, parietal,
  frontal, insula, putamen, caudate). So **cortical grey-matter dysfunction**, not pure
  fibre disconnection, drives neglect — also supported by PWI showing subcortical lesions
  cause neglect only when they additionally hypoperfuse cortex.
- **Function of the network:** these regions provide redundant information about body
  position/motion in space and adjust body position relative to the external world —
  i.e. they build the egocentric representation whose disturbance is neglect.
