# vmPFC in decision making: judgment under uncertainty or per se? (Fellows & Farah 2007)

## TL;DR

vmPFC damage makes preference judgments **inconsistent even under certainty** (no risk), so
vmPFC is necessary for **value-based choice *per se***, not merely for handling uncertainty as
the gambling-task literature had implied.

## Topic context

A key lesion result on [[Value-Based Decision Making and the vmPFC]]; reframes the vmPFC deficit
from "risk/uncertainty" to "basic value judgement."

## Problem / question

Earlier studies (Iowa Gambling Task etc.) tied vmPFC to decisions under **uncertainty**. Is
that the true deficit, or is vmPFC needed for value judgement even **without** uncertainty?

## Approach / method

A **preference task under certainty**: simple pairwise "which do you prefer?" choices with no
risk. 21 frontal-damage patients (vmPFC vs vmPFC-sparing) vs 19 matched controls; measured
**consistency/transitivity** of preferences.

## Key findings

- **vmPFC-damage patients made significantly more inconsistent (intransitive) preferences**
  (e.g. A>B, B>C, but C>A).
- Patients with frontal damage **sparing vmPFC performed normally**.
- Therefore vmPFC is necessary for **certain as well as uncertain** value-based decisions —
  judgement *per se*.

## Limitations / open questions

Frontal lesions vary; "inconsistency" is one operationalisation of impaired value judgement.
Doesn't address goal-directed vs habitual control (see
[[Goal-directed decision-making after vmPFC lesions - Reber et al 2017]]).
