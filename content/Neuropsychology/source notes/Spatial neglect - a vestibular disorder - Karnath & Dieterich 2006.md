# Spatial neglect — a vestibular disorder? (Karnath & Dieterich, Brain 2006)

## TL;DR

Neglect and unilateral vestibular dysfunction look alike (rightward eye/head bias) and
share right-hemisphere temporo-insular anatomy. But neglect is **not** a vestibular
disorder in the strict sense — those regions are **multisensory**, not "primary vestibular
cortex." Neglect is a disturbance of the **multisensory integration** that builds
egocentric space; the vestibular channel is just one input feeding it.

## Topic context

Explains *why* caloric/galvanic vestibular stimulation modulates neglect, and grounds the
transformation hypothesis in a real cortical substrate (the "vestibular" multisensory
network overlaps the neglect network).

## Method / argument structure

Review pairing (i) behavioural parallels and (ii) anatomical parallels between vestibular
disorders and neglect.

## Key findings

- **Behavioural parallel:** unilateral vestibular loss (or asymmetric caloric/galvanic
  stimulation) produces a tonic horizontal eye/head bias toward the affected side — just
  like the rightward bias of neglect. Left-cold-caloric stimulation shifts a neglect
  patient's search back toward midline; the same stimulation induces neglect-*like* search
  in healthy people.
- **Anatomical parallel:** the human "vestibular" cortical areas — **posterior insula/
  retroinsular (PIVC homologue), superior temporal gyrus, TPJ, IPL** — overlap the regions
  lesioned in neglect, and both show **right-hemisphere dominance** (in right-handers).
- **But these are multisensory, not purely vestibular.** No neuron responds to vestibular
  input *only*; the same cells answer to somatosensory, visual, optokinetic, auditory
  input. So the region is a **multisensory integrator**, not a primary vestibular cortex.
- **Conclusion:** the rightward orienting bias of neglect reflects a disturbed
  **transformation of converging vestibular + neck-proprioceptive + visual + auditory
  input into higher-order egocentric space**. This is why sensory manipulations
  (vestibular stimulation, neck vibration) are candidate **rehabilitation routes**.
