# Anterior cingulate conflict monitoring and adjustments in control (Kerns et al. 2004)

## TL;DR

Direct fMRI evidence for the monitor→adjust loop: **dACC conflict activity on one trial
predicts greater DLPFC activity and behavioural adjustment on the next**, confirming that the
ACC signals a need for more control which the lateral PFC then implements.

## Topic context

The empirical demonstration behind [[Conflict Monitoring and the Anterior Cingulate Cortex]] —
it closes the loop the conflict-monitoring hypothesis predicts.

## Problem / question

It was disputed whether conflict actually *causes* subsequent control adjustments. Does
trial-by-trial ACC conflict activity predict later control and behaviour?

## Approach / method

Stroop task with event-related fMRI, exploiting the **conflict-adaptation (Gratton) effect** —
better performance on high-conflict trials that follow high-conflict trials — and testing
cross-trial relationships between ACC, DLPFC, and behaviour.

## Key findings

- **ACC conflict-related activity on trial *n*** predicted **greater DLPFC activity on trial
  *n+1***.
- It also predicted **greater behavioural adjustment** (post-conflict slowing + accuracy gain).
- Together: ACC **monitors conflict** → lateral PFC **increases control** → behaviour improves.

## Limitations / open questions

Correlational across trials (not causal manipulation of ACC); the conflict-monitoring account is
later subsumed as one input to the broader EVC theory
([[The expected value of control - Shenhav et al 2013]]).
