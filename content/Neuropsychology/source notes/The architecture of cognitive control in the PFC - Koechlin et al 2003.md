# The architecture of cognitive control in the human PFC (Koechlin et al. 2003)

## TL;DR

Lateral PFC implements a **top-down cascade** of three nested control levels along the
rostro-caudal axis: **sensory** (premotor) → **contextual** (caudal LPFC) → **episodic**
(rostral LPFC), each scaling with the **information** its control signals must convey.

## Topic context

The empirical cornerstone of [[Hierarchical Cognitive Control]] — the cascade model of lateral
PFC organisation.

## Problem / question

What is the functional architecture of lateral PFC? Previous studies gave inconsistent
functional divisions; can a single model organise them?

## Approach / method

fMRI with tasks that **independently varied** three information sources — the stimulus, the
present perceptual context, and the temporal episode — to map each onto distinct PFC regions.
Control demand quantified in **Shannon-information** terms.

## Key findings

- **Three nested levels, three regions:**
  - **Sensory control** (stimulus → response) → **premotor cortex**.
  - **Contextual control** (context selects the S–R mapping) → **caudal LPFC** (BA 9/44/45).
  - **Episodic control** (temporal episode/goals select the task set) → **rostral LPFC** (BA 46).
- Control flows as a **top-down cascade** from rostral → caudal → premotor.
- Each region's activation scales with the **information** its control signals convey (more
  uncertainty resolved → more activation): $I_{\text{stim}}$, $I_{\text{cont}}$, $I_{\text{cues}}$.

## Limitations / open questions

Imaging shows **correlation**, not necessity — the causal/lesion test comes from
[[Hierarchical cognitive control deficits after frontal damage - Badre et al 2009]]. The
abstraction levels are operationalised specifically and may not generalise to all control tasks.
