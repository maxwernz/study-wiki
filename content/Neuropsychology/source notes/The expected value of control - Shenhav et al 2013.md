# The expected value of control (Shenhav et al. 2013)

## TL;DR

An integrative theory: the **dorsal ACC** computes the **Expected Value of Control (EVC)** —
balancing expected payoff, required control, and the cost of cognitive effort — to decide
**whether, where, and how much control** to allocate. This unifies dACC's many reported roles.

## Topic context

The unifying framework on [[Conflict Monitoring and the Anterior Cingulate Cortex]]; recasts
conflict monitoring as **one input** to a cost–benefit control-allocation computation.

## Problem / question

dACC has been tied to pain, reward, error, value, effort, monitoring, action selection — too
many functions. Is there a single computation that explains them?

## Approach / method

A normative/theoretical review proposing EVC and showing how it accounts for the diverse dACC
findings.

## Key findings

- The dACC's function is to compute **EVC** and use it to specify a control signal.
- EVC integrates three factors: **expected payoff** of a controlled process, **amount of
  control** needed, and the **intrinsic cost of cognitive effort**.
- This explains why dACC tracks reward, effort, conflict, and errors — all are **terms or
  inputs** in the same allocation calculation.
- dACC thus decides **whether, where, and how much** control to deploy; lateral PFC executes it.
