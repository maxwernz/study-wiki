# Inability to empathize: brain lesions that disrupt empathy (Hillis 2014)

## TL;DR

A lesion-based review of empathy: focal damage dissociates two systems — an **early "emotional
contagion"** system (sharing feelings) and a **later "cognitive perspective-taking"** system
(inferring them) — grounding empathy in identifiable frontal/limbic circuitry that imaging alone
can't establish.

## Topic context

The **necessity** (lesion) complement to imaging models on
[[Medial Frontal Cortex and Social Cognition]]; the affective/interpersonal face of
[[Frontal Lobe Syndrome and Phineas Gage]].

## Problem / question

Functional imaging shows where empathy *correlates* with activity, not what is *necessary*.
Which brain areas are necessary for emotional empathy, as revealed by focal lesions?

## Approach / method

Review bringing together scattered small focal-lesion (mainly stroke) studies of impaired
empathy, set against functional-imaging findings; uses Shamay-Tsoory's two-system model.

## Key findings

- Two dissociable empathy systems: **emotional contagion** (early; sharing/recognising feelings)
  vs **cognitive perspective-taking** (later; inferring others' states) — operating in parallel
  on **distinct neural substrates**.
- These **doubly dissociate** after lesions (one can be lost while the other is spared),
  implicating ventral/inferior-frontal + limbic circuitry for contagion and (ventro)medial
  prefrontal mentalising circuitry for perspective-taking.
- Methodological thesis: **lesion studies are essential** to move from correlation to necessity
  in social neuroscience.

substrates of each system remain approximate.
