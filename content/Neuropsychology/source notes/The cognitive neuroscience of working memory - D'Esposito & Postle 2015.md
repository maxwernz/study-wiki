# The cognitive neuroscience of working memory (D'Esposito & Postle 2015)

## TL;DR

Working memory is **attention directed at internal representations**: content is held in the
**same posterior regions that process it**, while the **PFC controls** the system by biasing
salience and adjudicating rules — "the control of the controller," gated by PFC–striatal
dopamine circuits.

## Topic context

The modern, state-dependent reframing of WM used in
[[Working Memory and the Prefrontal Cortex]]; shifts the field away from "PFC as storage box."

## Problem / question

What are the cognitive and neural mechanisms of WM — where is information stored, and what
does the PFC actually contribute?

## Approach / method

Review of cognitive models and human neuroimaging (esp. multivariate/decoding fMRI), plus
neural plausibility of state-based accounts.

## Key findings

- **Encoding = allocating attention** to internal representations (long-term-memory codes,
  sensory traces, motor plans).
- **Content is stored in modality-specific posterior cortex** (sensorimotor recruitment), not
  in a dedicated PFC buffer — decoding finds remembered stimuli in their processing regions.
- **PFC = controller:** biases the salience of mnemonic representations and selects among
  competing, context-dependent rules.
- The "control of the controller" emerges from **PFC–striatal circuits + ascending
  dopaminergic** signals (dopamine gates when to update vs protect WM contents).
