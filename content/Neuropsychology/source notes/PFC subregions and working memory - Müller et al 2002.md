# PFC subregions and working memory: lesion evidence (Müller et al. 2002)

## TL;DR

Human focal-lesion data show **distributed** lateral-PFC involvement in working memory: small
dorsal-lateral *or* ventromedial-orbital lesions don't impair WM; only **large lesions spanning
ventral + dorsal lateral PFC** do — and then **spatial WM suffers more than object WM**.

## Topic context

The lesion test of competing PFC-organisation theories, used in
[[Working Memory and the Prefrontal Cortex]]: "type of information" (domain) vs "type of
process," and the where/what extension into PFC.

## Problem / question

Are PFC subregions specialised by **stimulus category** (spatial vs object, à la
Goldman-Rakic) or is lateral-PFC WM function distributed? Lesions test necessity directly.

## Approach / method

Patients with focal damage to PFC subregions (dorsolateral, ventrolateral, ventromedial/
orbital) performed maintenance + monitoring tasks for **object** and **spatial** information;
performance compared by lesion location.

## Key findings

- Lesions confined to **dorsal lateral PFC** *or* **ventromedial orbital PFC** did **not**
  impair maintenance/monitoring of object or spatial info.
- Only **larger lesions involving both ventral and dorsal lateral PFC** impaired WM.
- When impaired, **spatial deficits were more severe than object deficits**.
- Conclusion: **distributed** localisation of WM function in lateral PFC — partial support for
  a spatial-bias dorsally, but **against a strict dorsal=spatial / ventral=object dichotomy**.

## Limitations / open questions

Focal human lesions are rarely clean; group sizes modest. Doesn't adjudicate the process
theory in detail, only argues against strict domain segregation.
