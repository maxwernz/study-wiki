# The anatomy of spatial neglect (Karnath & Rorden, Neuropsychologia 2012)

## TL;DR

Neglect looks anatomically "heterogeneous" only because the label is used as a catch-all.
If you separate the **core deficit** (egocentric biased gaze/search + lack of awareness)
from **satellite symptoms**, a clean anatomy emerges: the core deficit maps onto a right
**perisylvian network**; **allocentric/line-bisection** deficits map more **posterior/
inferior**; **extinction** maps onto the **temporo-parietal junction (TPJ)**.

## Topic context

Decades of lesion studies named many regions for "neglect," producing apparent chaos. The
authors' thesis: the chaos comes from (i) pooling distinct syndromes, (ii) mixing acute vs
chronic lesions, and (iii) mixing structural vs functional imaging.

## Method / argument structure

A review re-reading the lesion literature through the lens of symptom dissociations, plus
voxelwise lesion-behaviour mapping (VLBM) results from large acute-stroke cohorts.

## Key findings

- **Core neglect = perisylvian network.** Three cortical hubs straddling the sylvian
  fissure: **superior/middle temporal cortex + insula, inferior parietal lobule / TPJ,
  ventrolateral (inferior/middle) frontal cortex**, linked by white-matter tracts (SLF
  II/III, SOF, AF, EmC/IOF, MdLF). See [[Perisylvian Network for Spatial Orienting]].
- **Subcortical neglect is really cortical.** Basal-ganglia (caudate, putamen) and
  pulvinar lesions cause neglect by **hypoperfusing** structurally intact perisylvian
  cortex (shown with perfusion-weighted imaging, PWI).
- **Dissociations with clean anatomy:**
  - **Line bisection / allocentric (object-based) neglect** → more **posterior & inferior**
    (e.g. parahippocampal, middle/inferior temporal). 40% of core-neglect patients are
    *normal* on line bisection → behaviourally dissociable.
  - **Visual extinction** → **TPJ** (confirmed by TMS over right TPJ causing
    extinction-like performance).
- **Acute vs chronic matters.** ~2/3 of acute neglect recovers by ~1.3 years; superior/
  middle temporal cortex + basal ganglia predict *persistent* neglect. Chronic scans
  distort lesion size (tissue resorption), so acute imaging (DWI <48h, then T2/FLAIR) is
  preferred for anatomy.
- **Structural vs functional imaging** each miss things (disconnection, diaschisis,
  misery perfusion); combine modalities.
